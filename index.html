Fish curry Making process

The exact process of making fish curry can vary depending on the recipe and the region, but here is a general overview of the steps involved

Ingredients:

  • 500g fish (any firm white fish like cod, haddock, tilapia, or salmon)
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated or chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 tbsp. vegetable oil
  • 1 tsp. cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp. coriander powder
  • 1 tsp. turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp. chili powder
  • 1 cup water
  • Salt, to taste
  • Cilantro leaves, chopped, for garnish

How to cook Fish curry

Clean and cut the fish into bite-sized pieces. Sprinkle with a little salt and set aside.
Heat the oil in a large pan over medium-high heat. Add the cumin seeds and let them sizzle for a few seconds.
Add the chopped onion and cook until it becomes translucent and lightly browned.
Add the minced garlic and grated ginger and cook for another minute until fragrant.
Add the chopped tomatoes and cook for a few minutes until they become soft and mushy.
Add the coriander powder, turmeric powder, and chili powder and stir to combine.
Add the water and stir to make a smooth sauce. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and let it simmer for 5-10 minutes.
Add the fish to the pan and gently stir to coat it with the sauce. Cover the pan and let it simmer for 5-10 minutes until the fish is cooked through.
Adjust the seasoning with salt to taste.
Garnish with chopped cilantro leaves and serve hot with rice or bread. Enjoy your delicious fish curry!

Mexicana taco Making process

Lime is probably the most essential of authentic Mexican taco toppings. A squeeze of lime adds a dash of acid that brings out the other flavors in a taco beautifully. Onions, cilantro, pico de gallo, sliced radishes or cucumbers, avocado, and chili peppers are also standard toppings for authentic tacos.

Ingredients:

  • Corn tortillas
  • Protein: beef, chicken, pork, fish or beans
  • Seasonings for the protein: cumin, chili powder, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper
  • Toppings: diced tomatoes, diced onions, cilantro, shredded lettuce, guacamole, and cheese
  • Salsa: red or green salsa, or pico de gallo
  • Lime wedges (to squeeze over the taco)

How to cook Mexicana taco

Preheat a skillet or grill pan over medium-high heat.
Season the protein with cumin, chili powder, garlic powder, onion powder, salt, and pepper.
Cook the protein in the skillet or grill pan until browned and cooked through.
Warm the tortillas in a separate skillet or in the oven.
Assemble the tacos by placing the cooked protein on the warm tortillas.
Add toppings such as diced tomatoes, diced onions, cilantro, shredded lettuce, guacamole, and cheese.
Drizzle with salsa and a squeeze of lime.

Paneer curry Making process

Paneer curry is a delicious vegetarian dish from Indian cuisine. Here are the ingredients you will need.

Ingredients:

  • Paneer - 200-250g
  • Onion - 1 medium, chopped
  • Tomatoes - 2 medium, chopped
  • Ginger - 1 inch piece, grated
  • Garlic - 3-4 cloves, minced
  • Green chili - 1-2, chopped
  • Turmeric powder - 1/2 tsp
  • Red chili powder - 1/2 tsp
  • Coriander powder - 1 tsp
  • Garam masala - 1/2 tsp
  • Cumin seeds - 1/2 tsp
  • Salt - to taste
  • Oil - 2-3 tbsp
  • Water - 1/2 to 1 cup
  • Fresh coriander leaves - for garnishing

How to cook Panner curry

Cut the paneer into cubes and set aside.
Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter.
Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
Add grated ginger, minced garlic, and chopped green chili. Sauté for a minute.
Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and mushy.
Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, garam masala, and salt. Mix well and cook for a couple of minutes.
Add the paneer cubes and stir gently. Add water as required to adjust the consistency of the curry.
Cover the pan and simmer the curry for 5-7 minutes or until the paneer is cooked and the curry has thickened to your desired consistency.
Turn off the heat and garnish with fresh coriander leaves.
Serve hot with rice or Indian bread such as naan or roti.
